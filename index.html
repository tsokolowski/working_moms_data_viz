There are significantly fewer women in leadership positions than men.

In 2018, only

1

in

5

corporate CEOs are women.

Many people believe this is because women choose to focus on childcare and being a mom.

In reality only

2%

of women plan to leave the workforce for family reasons.

But whether she wants them to or not, these plans often have to change once a woman has a child and goes back to work.

43%

of women scale back at work after having a child.

And

27%

quit their job at some point to care for children.

This has an enormous impact on a woman’s career and leadership opportunities.

In a recent study, women who took 1 year off from work earned

39%

less than those that didn’t.

And those that took 4 or more years off earned

65%

less.

How might we support new moms as they transition back to work so they can achieve their goals both in their career and as a mom?

A project by Taylor Sokolowski in Fall 2018 for the course Data Visualization I in the Design for Social Innovation MFA program at the School of Visual Arts. Data sources: Women in the Workplace, McKinsey and Company, and The Slowly Narrowing Gender Wage Gap, Institute for Women's Policy Research.
